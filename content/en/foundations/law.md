Over text and images AI makes, and decisions AI hands down, the question "whose is this, and who is responsible?" is growing fast. Law is the set of rules that draws those boundaries, so it becomes the standard for telling **what is allowed and what is not**.

Law isn't about memorizing statutes. It's an old design for how to make people keep their promises to one another. It's the work of settling in advance who can have what, and who takes responsibility when things go wrong.

## What it covers

- **Rights** — These are the share a person holds that others can't trespass on. How far your writing, images, and data are yours gets settled here.
- **Responsibility** — It's who carries it when something goes wrong. When a decision an AI made causes harm, where that responsibility goes is still a question being argued over.
- **Contracts** — These are the device that binds people to keep a promise. Making clear what each side agreed to give and take cuts down on disputes.
- **Regulation** — These are the lines of prohibition society draws in advance. Too tight and they block new attempts; too loose and they let the harm grow.
- **Limits of what is allowed** — It's the boundary of how far you may go. Being able to do something and being allowed to do it are different matters.

## Why it matters more in the AI era

Who holds the copyright, where the responsibility goes, which data you're allowed to use — technology runs fast and law is still catching up behind it. So right now, **the boundary of what's allowed is itself the real limit of business and research**. People who can read that boundary save themselves wasted steps.

## Where it leads

Law is the values and ethics of [Philosophy](../philosophy/) translated into institutions, and regulation and responsibility change the incentives of [Economics](../economics/) to move the choices people make. On questions of safety and responsibility, it also meets [Engineering](../engineering/).
