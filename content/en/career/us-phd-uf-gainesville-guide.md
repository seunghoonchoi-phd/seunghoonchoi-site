## Core Idea

U.S. PhD preparation has two phases. Before admission, the focus is the lab, professor, documents, tests, and interviews. After admission, the focus shifts to visa, entry, housing, money, phone, and administration.

For someone coming to UF, Gainesville itself matters. It is not a large city where every option stays open late. Housing, transportation, first-week supplies, and rent payment should be checked before departure.

The F-1 visa is not only an entry document. After arrival, the student has to maintain status. Enrollment, address, employment, and travel records can become important later.

Money is especially confusing at the beginning. Before a U.S. bank account is open, card payments, international transfers, fees, and autopay settings need one more careful check.

## Questions to Ask

- Am I mixing pre-admission preparation with post-admission settlement work.
- Which tasks must be finished before departure, and which can wait until arrival.
- Have I checked both school rules and government rules for F-1 status.
- Can my housing and rent payment start safely before I have a U.S. bank account.
- How will I handle transportation, phone, and basic supplies in my first week in Gainesville.

These questions make the move less vague. Admission is not the end. It is the next stage of actually starting life in the United States.
