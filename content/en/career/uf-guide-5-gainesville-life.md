Once your admission, visa, and administrative setup are done, real life begins. Where to live, how to get around, how much it costs per month, where to go when you're sick, what to grab when a hurricane is coming. This guide breaks down actual day-to-day life in Gainesville, Florida (University of Florida) into housing, transit, monthly budget, health, climate, community, and travel.

## Housing: Almost Everyone Lives Off-Campus

Nearly all UF grad students live off-campus. On-campus grad/family housing (Corry, Tanglewood, Diamond Village) has a 2-3 year waitlist, so it's effectively unrealistic for incoming students.

Student rentals are usually furnished + by-the-bed, which means roommates are auto-assigned. If you want a place to yourself, you'll need a studio or 1BR.

Here are the candidate complexes and search tools:

| Category | Details |
|---|---|
| Notable complexes | Hideaway (15-20 min by bus, [hideawaygainesville.com](https://hideawaygainesville.com/)), Lexington Crossing, Gainesville Place |
| Unified search | [housing.offcampus.ufl.edu](https://housing.offcampus.ufl.edu/) · [Swamp Rentals](https://www.swamprentals.com/) |
| Popular area | SW 20th Ave / Archer Rd corridor (32608): good value + direct RTS access + shopping (Trader Joe's, Sam's Club) |

Two things to take care of when you sign:

- Photograph any existing damage at move-in; the surest way to avoid deposit disputes.
- Renters insurance ($10-20/month): many complexes require it.

## Transit: RTS Buses Are Free for Students

One of Gainesville's biggest advantages is transportation cost.

- All RTS routes are free with your Gator 1 card ([taps.ufl.edu](https://taps.ufl.edu/)). Use the GNV RideRTS or Transit app.
- Bike and scooter culture is strong (UF is a Gold Bicycle Friendly University). E-scooters: Bird, Spin, Veo.

Do you actually need a car? If you live in the campus or Archer corridor, you can get by your first year with no car, just buses and a bike. A car is handy for big-box stores, H-Mart (Orlando), and weekend trips, so consider a used car once you've settled in. A campus parking decal runs about $160/year.

## Monthly Budget: Living on the Stipend

Taking a typical GA stipend of ~$32k/year (~$2,667/month pre-tax), here's roughly what monthly spending looks like with a roommate:

| Item | Monthly (sharing with a roommate) |
|---|---|
| Rent (by-the-bed, some utilities included) | $700-$950 |
| Utilities + internet (if not included) | $100-$180 |
| Groceries | $300-$400 |
| Phone (MVNO) | $25-$50 |
| Transit (bus free) | $0-$30 |
| Other | $150-$250 |
| Total | about $1,300-$1,850 |

Take-home pay is roughly $2,200-$2,400/month, so living alone is possible but tight. The key savings levers are sharing with a roommate + free RTS. Going solo in a studio means about half your stipend goes to housing.

## Groceries and Shopping

- General stores: Publix (BOGO deals), Aldi (low-cost), Walmart, Trader Joe's (3724 SW Archer Rd), Sam's Club (SW 30th Pl). Note: there's no Costco in Gainesville.
- Korean groceries: There's no full Korean supermarket in town. Most needs are covered by pan-Asian markets like Chun Ching Market (418 NW 8th Ave) plus online Weee! ([sayweee.com](https://www.sayweee.com/)), with a once-a-month run to H-Mart Orlando (1.5-2 hours by car) for serious stocking up.

## Health and Medical Care (SHCC): The Essentials

The medical system is structured differently from Korea's, so getting the concepts straight early saves a lot of money and time.

### Two Separate Pockets: Health Fee vs. Insurance

UF medical costs split into two pockets:

- (A) Student Health Fee: included in tuition. As an enrolled student, SHCC office visits themselves are free and unlimited (physician consults, phone consults, etc.).
- (B) Insurance (GatorGradCare): pays for separately billed items (labs, imaging, procedures, vaccines, prescriptions, physical therapy, etc.). SHCC is a GatorGradCare Tier 1 (cheapest) provider, so your out-of-pocket cost is minimized.

The takeaway is simple: for GatorGradCare members, getting care at SHCC is almost always the cheapest option. Billing questions: SHCC PFS (352) 273-4546 / insurance@shcc.ufl.edu.

### ★ The Waiver Trap: Get the Direction Wrong and You Pay More

A GA (.25 FTE+, 10+ hours/week) is auto-enrolled (Employee Only) in GatorGradCare (BlueCross BlueShield FL) when newly eligible. Your share is about $12/month (payroll deduction); UF covers the rest.

The most common mistake here is misunderstanding the direction of the waiver.

> ⚠️ Key point: If you don't waive, you're auto-enrolled in the school's mandatory plan (UnitedHealthcare StudentResources), and the premium ($1,000+ per semester) is charged to your student account. So a GA who has GatorGradCare must go through the "waive with GatorGradCare" process to block the UHC auto-enrollment and charge.

A waiver, once done, is valid for about 3 consecutive semesters (≈1 year), so watch for the renewal reminder before it expires. Process: [How to Waive (GatorGradCare)](https://healthcompliance.shcc.ufl.edu/health-insurance-requirement/gatorgradcare/how-to-waive-with-gatorgradcare/) · [GatorGradCare](https://gatorcare.org/gatorgradcare/) · [plan rates/dates](https://healthcompliance.shcc.ufl.edu/uf-plan-rates-dates/)

### ★ Continuity of Ongoing Medication

If you're on regular medication from Korea (for a chronic condition, psychiatric care, etc.), the goal is to continue it without interruption after arriving in the U.S. The essentials:

- SHCC can take over your existing treatment directly (or refer you to a community specialist if it can't).
- The helpful records are English-language medical summaries, prescription history, and prior test results. No official form is needed; it just has to be in English. More is better.
- Providers prefer to receive records before your first appointment, so send your records in advance. Submit to the SHCC Medical Records Office, and put your UFID on every record ([forms & records](https://shcc.ufl.edu/forms-records/)).
- Being enrolled comes first (after registration). It's best to reach out in the summer or right after arrival to minimize any gap.
- Before you leave Korea: prepare an English treatment summary, 6-12 months of prescription history, your medical chart, and any test results. Put your name in English on all documents and list medications by generic name.
- Bridge medication: to cover the period before handoff is complete, secure an adequate supply in Korea (typically up to a 90-day personal supply) and carry the original containers + English prescription/summary in your carry-on.
- ⚠️ Controlled substances are separate: U.S. campus health centers are conservative about benzodiazepines, stimulants, and the like. Whether SHCC continues these directly or refers you out, confirm after arrival by bringing your medication list to SHP. SHP (existing patients) (352) 273-3390 / shp@shcc.ufl.edu.

### Emergencies: Where to Go

The decision order is SHCC (weekdays, mild) → Urgent Care (mild to moderate) → ER (life-threatening, severe).

- UF Health Shands ER / Level I trauma center: 1515-1600 SW Archer Rd (24h)
- Springhill freestanding ER (24/7): 8475 NW 39th Ave, (352) 627-0400

Keep in mind: ER and ambulance are very expensive (hundreds to $1,000+ even after insurance). For mild cases, SHCC or urgent care is far cheaper. Mental health crisis 988 / CWC (352) 392-1575 / 911.

## Climate, Hurricanes, and Wildlife (TMI)

### Climate and Weather

Gainesville has a humid subtropical climate. The August average high is ~32.6°C (~91°F), with near-daily afternoon thunderstorms, short and intense. Winters are mild. A surprising hazard is strong indoor air conditioning, so pack 1-2 thin long-sleeve tops even for summer.

The core of an August arrival packing list: breathable summer clothes, a compact umbrella and waterproof jacket, and strong-sun protection (sunscreen, hat, sunglasses). Bring minimal heavy winter clothing.

### Hurricanes (June-November, peak September)

Gainesville is inland, so the main impact is high winds, power outages, and heavy rain rather than direct landfall. Still, don't get complacent (Helene caused major outages in Alachua in 2024).

Terms to know:

- Cone of uncertainty: the range of the projected path
- Evacuation order: voluntary (advisory) / mandatory (required). Check your zone at [FloridaDisaster.org](https://www.floridadisaster.org).

Supplies: 3 days of water (1 gallon/day), emergency food, flashlight, power bank, medications, cash, and a full tank of gas. And be sure to register for UF Alert texts.

### Wildlife and Bugs: Realities You Won't Find in Korea

- Alligators are real. They can be near any body of water, including Lake Alice on campus, so be careful walking near water, and don't feed them (it's illegal).
- Lovebugs (swarm in May and September; acidic, so wash your car promptly to protect the paint), fire ants (don't step on the dirt mounds), palmetto bugs (large roaches), and mosquitoes and no-see-ums (use DEET repellent).

## Korean Community: A Hub for Settlement Info

Gainesville isn't a huge city, so the Korean network offers real help from used-goods deals to settlement information.

- UF KSA (Korean Student Association): Instagram [@uf_ksa](https://www.instagram.com/uf_ksa/) · ufkorean.com, the key channel for used furniture, cars, roommates, and events.
- Korean churches: Gainesville Korean Baptist Church (5200 SW 63rd Blvd, [kbcg.org](https://kbcg.org/)) · Korean Presbyterian Church (3146 NW 13th St). They serve as hubs for settling in, meals, and networking.
- Korean restaurants: BeQue Holic (BBQ), Seoul Pocha, Bonchon, KPOT, and others around UF.
- Online: HeyKorean · MissyUSA · [K-Florida](https://www.kflorida.com/) · Naver cafés.

## Nearby Travel: Stress Relief

When studying wears you down, here are places worth a drive:

- Natural springs: a steady ~22°C year-round. Ginnie Springs, Devil's Den, and Ichetucknee (tubing) are the standouts and Gainesville's greatest strength. 45 minutes to 1 hour by car.
- Beaches: Cedar Key (1 hour, west) · St. Augustine (1.5 hours, the oldest city in the continental U.S., highly recommended) · Daytona (1.5 hours).
- Theme parks: Orlando (~2 hours, Disney and Universal) · Tampa (~2 hours) · Miami (~5 hours, a weekend trip).

That's the big picture of life in Gainesville. Get familiar with it and you'll cut down on trial and error from your very first month. More study-abroad and settlement resources continue at seunghoonchoi.com.
