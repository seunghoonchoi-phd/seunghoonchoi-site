## Core Idea

AI changes work with checkable answers first. Translation, summaries, calculation, and basic coding are easier to compare and score, so they face automation pressure earlier.

But company work does not end with the output. Authority, security, responsibility, approval, and field context have to move together. If the organization cannot process AI's answer, the work does not become faster.

Review should raise the floor. It should catch obvious errors, but if it forces every result into a bland template, it also removes the better outcomes AI could have produced.

The human role that remains is not just checking everything by hand. It is deciding what to delegate, bearing the result when something fails, and designing authority and standards inside the organization.

## Questions to Ask

- Is my work easy to check, or is responsibility and authority the bigger part.
- When AI creates an output, who reviews it before it becomes public.
- Can the organization actually give AI the data, authority, and tools it needs.
- Does the review standard catch errors, or does it also reduce the chance of a better result.
- If headcount falls, does cost fall too, or do context and relationships disappear with it.

These questions show that AI automation is not only a productivity tool. It is an operating decision about which work goes to machines and which responsibility stays with people.
