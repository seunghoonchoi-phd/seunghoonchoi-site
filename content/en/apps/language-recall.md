I used to save foreign language expressions in Notion and rarely look at them again. Whenever I had something new to say, I searched GPT for a similar expression, often more than once. I built this tool to find what I had already saved and put it to use before adding another sentence.

## Search saved expressions first

Enter what you want to say and the app searches saved expressions before anything else. It shows up to three close results. If one fits, the new search phrase is connected to the existing item instead of creating a duplicate. A new expression is added only when none of them fits.

## Use a new expression today and review it later

Say a new expression once more on the day you add it. The app then brings it back after 1, 3, 7, 14, 30, and 60 days. Practice is limited to five items a day. A short followup records whether you recalled it, read it from the screen, used it in real life, and were understood.

English cards show a Korean situation and ask for the English expression. Chinese cards show the Korean meaning and ask for the Chinese characters. Pinyin appears when the answer is revealed.

## The public demo contains no personal Notion data

The public demo uses newly written sample data, and learning records stay in the current browser. My personal Notion source text, token, page IDs, and search history are not included in the public files. The version connected to my personal Notion runs only in a separate private environment.
