I used to save foreign language expressions in Notion and rarely look at them again. Whenever I had something new to say, I searched GPT for a similar expression, often more than once. I built this tool to save a useful expression on the spot and bring the saved ones back into real use.

## Save a useful expression right away, and find saved ones first

On the search screen, the quick save box needs only two fields: the Korean situation and the expression to learn. The saved expression goes straight into today's review. Extra details such as the scene or the counterpart stay in a collapsed section until you need them. When you search for something to say, the app still shows up to three close results from your saved expressions, and if one fits, it connects the new search phrase to the existing item instead of creating a duplicate. If a new sentence looks similar to an existing item, the app asks for one duplicate confirmation before saving.

## Use a new expression today and review it before it fades

Say a new expression once more on the day you add it. The app then brings it back after 1, 3, 7, 14, 30, and 60 days. Practice is limited to five items per language a day, and a short followup records whether you recalled it, read it from the screen, and were understood. When today's review is empty, the app shows the review schedule for the next seven days instead.

The My expressions screen lists everything you saved, newest first. You can filter the list by language or text, open a card to edit its meaning and sentence, and archive expressions you no longer need.

English cards show a Korean situation and ask for the English expression. Chinese cards show the Korean meaning and ask for the Chinese characters. Pinyin appears when the answer is revealed.

## The public demo contains no personal Notion data

The public demo uses newly written sample data, and learning records stay in the current browser. My personal Notion source text, token, page IDs, and search history are not included in the public files. The version connected to my personal Notion runs only in a separate private environment.
